Climate Impact Drivers (CID)

Climate Impact Drivers are the physical climate conditions that directly influence human and natural systems. These include:

  • Temperature changes: Heatwaves, cold spells, rising temperatures
  • Precipitation patterns: Droughts, heavy rainfall, changing seasonal patterns
  • Extreme events: Typhoons, wildfires, storms
  • Gradual changes: Sea level rise, ocean acidification

Exposure

Exposure refers to the presence of people, livelihoods, environmental services, and resources in places that could be adversely affected by climate impacts.

  • Geographic location: Coastal areas, floodplains, urban centers
  • Infrastructure: Buildings, transportation networks, utilities
  • Economic activities: Agriculture, tourism, industry
  • Social systems: Communities, institutions, services

Vulnerability

Vulnerability is the propensity to be adversely affected by climate impacts. It encompasses sensitivity and adaptive capacity.

  • Social vulnerability: Age, health, income, education level
  • Economic vulnerability: Dependence on climate-sensitive sectors
  • Physical vulnerability: Infrastructure quality, building standards
  • Institutional vulnerability: Governance capacity, social networks

Climate Impacts

Climate impacts are the consequences of climate change on human and natural systems, resulting from the interaction of CID with exposure and vulnerability.

  • Health impacts: Heat stress, disease outbreaks, mental health effects
  • Economic impacts: Crop losses, infrastructure damage, tourism decline
  • Social impacts: Displacement, inequality, conflict
  • Environmental impacts: Ecosystem disruption, species loss

Adaptation Policies

Adaptation policies are deliberate actions to reduce vulnerability and enhance resilience to climate impacts.

  • Structural measures: Sea walls, improved drainage, climate-resilient infrastructure
  • Ecosystem-based: Green infrastructure, natural flood management
  • Social measures: Early warning systems, community education
  • Economic measures: Insurance, incentives, financial support
